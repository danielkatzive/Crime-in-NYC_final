Crime in the city by the numbers

It's more complicated than you think

By Daniel Katzive

Crime is on the rise in New York City! This is certainly a theme heard often in recent election campaings. But data from the New York City Police Department (NYPD) provides a more nuanced picture.

Crime has risen across most major felony categories from very low rates reached in the final years of the last decade, before the Covid epidemic hit. But the most violent of crimes, murder and rape, remain at fairly stable levels. In contrast, grand larceny has reached new highs this year, as has felony assault.

One explanation for the perception that crime is getting worse is that these categories of crime may be more noticeable to the public, particularly assault which seems increasingly to affect random victims rather than following more predictable patterns. Indeed, NYPD Deputy Inspector Naoki Yaguchi, commanding officer of the 24th Precinct on the Upper West Side noted at a recent public meeting that, while crime numbers are lower recently in his precinct, “we have had a number of random assaults taking place.”
